Swinging a compass

So you know which direction you're actually pointing in.

Overview

Swinging a compass is what you do to calibrate a compass. It allows you to work out what effect magnetic materials on your boat are having on your compass and correct for them.

There are lots of different ways to swing a compass, many listed and explained below.

The basic premise is the same for all of them. Point the boat in a known direction and then see what the compass says. The difference between the correct heading and the compass tells you the deviation on that heading. Several deviations are collected together into FIXME blah blah
